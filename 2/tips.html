A Semicolon; a Question Mark? An Exclamation Mark!

You may have noticed sentences from last lesson ends in a “comma”, and a subscript 9 — or an empty comma — appearing where you’d expect a comma. Yes, those are periods and commas in Patchélogue!

Just like Greek and Ethiopian, Patchélogue has a different set of punctuations. While Greek use “;” for question marks, Patchélogue use it for exclamation marks! There’s even a “exclamation comma” for sentences like “Hey, you!”, which looked like a empty semicolon: “⨟”.
