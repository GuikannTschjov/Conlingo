A Semicolon; a Question Mark? An Exclamation Mark!

You may have noticed sentences from last lesson ends in a “comma” like unfinished, and a subscript 9 — or an empty comma — appearing in where you’d expect a comma. Yes, those are periods and commas in Patchélogue!

Just like Greek and Ethiopian, Patchélogue has a different set of punctuations. While Greek use “;” for question marks, and Patchélogue use it for exclamation marks! There’s even a “exclamation comma” for sentences like “Hey, you!” that looked like a empty semicolon: “⨟”.
