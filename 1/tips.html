About Patchélogue

Patchélogue is a constructed language created as a hobby, by Kaihan Zhang, a Chinese student. It’s set to be spoken in a vast and powerful empire in an parallel universe. Cliché, innit?

Since it’s speakers are in a parallel universe where latin letters never existed, they don’t actually call their language by this western-y name “Patchélogue”. No, that’s just the author’s fancy anglolization of the phonetic transcription “Patshélog”.

And, yes, the language is named after Patchouli Knowledge.

When “y” Means “f” — Patchélogue Letters

Patchélogue is written in an alphabet called “Higilaya”. It was created by a group of ancient barbarians who, conincidentally, discovered some English comic books left by a time-traveller. That’s why there are letters identical to Latin ones and pronounced differently.

The biggest feature of Higilaya letters is that, when turned upside-down, every letter became another letter. For example: turn “ʎ⟨m⟩” upside-down and you get “y⟨f⟩”. It’s like Arabic numeral “9” and “6” or Latin letter “q” and “b” but taken to eleven.

Plenty Plurals

In this course, you add a -ʎ⟨-m⟩ to make a noun plural, but it’s not always like that. For “unconscious” nouns, you add a -q⟨-s⟩. You’ll learn this grammar aspect and the letter later.
