About Patchélogue

Patchélogue(/ˌpʌˈtʃeɪlɒg/, Vżɑƞı̣nṣK/pʰɐˈtʃʰɛlok̚/, lit.“Language of patchouli”) is an constructed language created as a hobby, by Kaihan Zhang, a Chinese student. It’s set to be spoken in a vast and powerful empire in an alternative universe. Cliché, innit?

Since it’s speakers are in an alternative universe where latin letters didn’t exist, they don’t actually call their language by this western-y name “Patchélogue”. No, that’s just the author’s fancy anglolization of the phonetic transcription “Patshélog”.

And, yes, the language is named after Patchouli Knowledge.

When “y” means “f” — Patchélogue letters

Patchélogue is written in Higilaya(ᖷiĸinżıZ̄/çic⁼iˈlaja/, lit."Script of saint"), which looks like Latin letters, because it was created after a group of ancient barbarians discovered some English comic books a time-traveller left.

The biggest feature of Higilaya letters is that, when turned upside-down, every letter became another letter. For example: turn “ʎ⟨m⟩” upside-down and you get “y⟨f⟩”. It’s like Arabic numeral “9” and “6” or Latin letter “q” and “b” but taken to eleven, except “q” and “b” are pronounced /s/ and /n/ in Patchélogue, as you’ll learn later.
